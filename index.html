Master the Art of Brewing

Follow our simple guides to extract the full flavor potential of your beans.

V60 Pour Over Guide

This method highlights clean, complex, and light-bodied notes. Ideal for showcasing floral single-origins.

Ratio 1 : 16
Grind Size Medium-Fine
Water Temp 93°C (200°F)
Time 3:00 mins

Step-by-step Process:

  1. Prepare: Rinse filter paper with hot water, discard water. Add 20g of ground coffee and tap to level.
  2. The Bloom: Pour 40g of water, wet all grounds evenly. Wait 35 seconds to let the gases escape.
  3. Main Pour: Pour in circular motions up to 150g, maintaining a slow and steady stream.
  4. Final Pour: Once drained slightly, pour again up to 320g. Give the dripper a gentle swirl and let it drip fully.

French Press Guide

This method yields a robust, full-bodied, and rich cup. Great for bringing out chocolatey and earthy flavors.

Ratio 1 : 15
Grind Size Coarse
Water Temp 95°C (203°F)
Time 4:00 mins

Step-by-step Process:

  1. Dose: Add 30g of coarsely ground coffee to your empty French press container.
  2. Wet: Pour in 450g of hot water, making sure all coffee grounds are fully saturated.
  3. Steep: Place the lid on top (don't plunge yet) and let it steep for exactly 4 minutes.
  4. Plunge & Pour: Gently and slowly press the plunger down. Immediately decant into cups to avoid over-extraction.
